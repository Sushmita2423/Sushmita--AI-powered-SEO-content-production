## How LLMs (actually) work 

An LLM is a really sophisticated autocomplete. 

It reads a vast amount of text from the web. 

* Articles
* Forums
* Books
* Code
* YouTube video transcripts

It learns which words tend to follow which other words. 

When you ask ChatGPT a question, it predicts the next word based on probability. 

If everyone online is saying "the best way to get more clients online is with good SEO," that's the phrase the model statistically generates. 

LLMs learn which words tend to follow which other words based on probability 

Which means: 

* There's no library inside ChatGPT
* There's no database
* There's no ranked list of best sources

What's left after training is a frozen statistical snapshot. A mathematical model of how language tends to behave. 

That single fact changes everything about AI search. 

## The 7 most common “AEO” myths 

Some of these don't just not work, they can actively damage your SEO. 

1. AI is killing search. Search engines: 81B monthly sessions. LLMs: 45B (56% the size of search worldwide). Combined search usage is up 26% since ChatGPT launched in 2022 ([Graphite](https://www.linkedin.com/redir/redirect?url=https%3A%2F%2Fgraphite%2Eio%2Ffive-percent%2Fai-is-much-bigger-than-you-think&urlhash=49WG&trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse%5Flittle-text-block)). AI is expanding search, not killing it.
2. You need schema on every page. LLMs don't parse schema markup the way Google's SERP features do. Use schema for SEO. Don't pay anyone to repackage it as a magical new AEO tactic.
3. You need to write in a special AI-friendly way. LLMs are trained on billions of examples of natural human language. They handle typos, slang, casual grammar. They were built to read how people actually write, not rigid copy. Write for humans, clearly, like always.
4. You need an llms.txt file. Every study suggests no impact whatsoever ([like this one](https://www.linkedin.com/redir/redirect?url=https%3A%2F%2Ftrakkr%2Eai%2Ftrakkr-research%2Fllmstxt-effect&urlhash=efwn&trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse%5Flittle-text-block)). Add it if you want, it's free. Don't pay anyone to implement it as part of an "AEO strategy."
5. Create a .md version of every page. 0 requests from any LLM crawlers. Even when .md files are explicitly listed in llms.txt, they aren't fetched. Worse, a public .md version makes scraping your content cheaper (lower bandwidth, easier parsing) and adds cannibalisation risk.
6. Add "Summarise with AI" buttons that inject prompts. Microsoft has classified this as “[AI Recommendation Poisoning](https://www.linkedin.com/redir/redirect?url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww%2Emicrosoft%2Ecom%2Fen-us%2Fsecurity%2Fblog%2F2026%2F02%2F10%2Fai-recommendation-poisoning%2F&urlhash=6oq%5F&trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse%5Flittle-text-block),” a threat technique. Mitigations are being deployed.
7. Publish self-serving listicles at scale. Google's reviews system is crushing these pages, and SEO penalties directly result in AI citation loss.

## The ONLY 2 ways you show up in AI answers 

There are two ways to show up in an LLM. 

And two alone. 

1\. Becoming part of the training data and 2\. Being retrieved in search engines 

### Way 1: Training data

Your brand gets baked into the model. 

This happens if you’re mentioned often across authoritative sources like: 

* TechCrunch
* G2
* Capterra
* Reddit threads
* HubSpot comparison articles…

The patterns of language around your name embed into training, and the model generates your name like it would with any other word. 

Result: brand mention with no link. 

### Way 2: Retrieval

When you ask an LLM something current (news, pricing, product comparisons), training data isn't enough. 

The model runs a live web search, reads what it finds, and writes its answer from those results. 

Result: URL citation (and possible brand mention) with link. 

How you can get into either: 

* Earned coverage gets you into training data
* SEO rankings get you cited via retrieval

There’s literally no third path. 

## The 65/35 split 

A recent [Semrush study](https://www.linkedin.com/redir/redirect?url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww%2Esemrush%2Ecom%2Fblog%2Fchatgpt-search-insights%2F&urlhash=AvkF&trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse%5Flittle-text-block) found a 65.5 / 34.5 split: 

* 65.5% of answers come from training data only (no search)
* 34.5% involve a live web search

The model is leaning more on what it already "knows." 

LLMs use search 34.5% of the time and 65.5% from training data 

But [retrieval is growing](https://www.linkedin.com/redir/redirect?url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww%2Esearchenginejournal%2Ecom%2Fopenai-crawl-activity-tripled-since-gpt-5-data-shows%2F573316%2F&urlhash=Kdd3&trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse%5Flittle-text-block).

## The 10% that actually matters 

Two levers (and neither is new). 

1. Earned = Mentions. Focus on being referenced. Earn coverage so the model already knows you.
2. Owned = Content. Focus on being retrieved. Rank in search so LLMs cite you.

You can control earned mentions AND traditional SEO rankings 

### How to win on earned

AKA earned media, PR, backlinks. The kind of thing SEO has always needed. 

1. Identify the authoritative sources LLMs likely train on. Major industry publications, forums like Reddit, well-cited research, G2, Capterra, Trustpilot.
2. Pursue genuine coverage. Expert quotes, contributions, being cited in others' content.
3. Get on third-party comparison content. Listicles ranking in Google’s index.
4. Build entity consistency. Your brand description should match across LinkedIn, Crunchbase, G2, your site, and Wikipedia. Inconsistent data confuses the model.
5. Be patient. This is a slower lever, not a quick win. E.g. GPT-5.5's cutoff was August 2025\. It launched in March 2026\. That's a 6-month gap before earned coverage shows up in training data.

Training data is a slower lever to pull 

### How to win on owned

AKA SEO, with a more intentional focus. 

1. Identify the conversational queries your audience asks LLMs. Use traditional keyword tools, turn keywords into questions, reverse engineer competitor sources.
2. Map those to pages on your site. Create them and genuinely answer them.
3. Rank those pages in Google.
4. Build content clusters, not isolated pages. Fan-out queries reward depth.
5. Monitor visibility, crawler analytics, and brand uplift. Use a tool like [Mentions](https://www.linkedin.com/redir/redirect?url=https%3A%2F%2Fmentions%2Eso%2F&urlhash=WlT7&trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse%5Flittle-text-block). Then double down where you're seeing success.

How your owned content gets cited by LLMs 

Owned and earned are not separate, they compound together. 

Owned content gets you cited. Earned mentions get you remembered. 

The brands winning AI search right now are doing both (very well). 

## The dark search problem 

Tracking used to be simple: Google Search Console + Google Analytics. 

But dark search is now darker than ever. 

A typical buyer journey in 2026: 

1. They see your brand in a Google AI Overview
2. They scroll LinkedIn and read a post about you
3. They ask ChatGPT for alternatives
4. They read the comparison thread on Reddit
5. They check reviews on G2

All invisible to your tracking. 

Then they Google your brand name and book a demo. 

The only thing your analytics ever sees is "branded traffic." 

5 touchpoints with 1 trackable click. 

Visibility is the metric you actually need to track now.
